A Complete 2025 to 2027 Playbook to Prepare Your Bank for AMLA Direct Supervision and Group Wide Oversight

The EU Anti-Money Laundering Authority launches direct supervision in July 2027, with authority to fine up to ‚Ç¨10 million or 10% of annual turnover. Unlike national supervision, AMLA will conduct cross-border, group-wide inspections and demand complete data lineage for every risk decision.

Data Infrastructure Audit 
AMLA's first target will be data quality gaps. Most banks find 12-18% of retail customers have incomplete records, inconsistent risk ratings, or missing beneficial ownership data. You need demonstrable data lineage showing exactly how risk decisions are made. This can't be built last-minute.

Phase 2: Implementation (Months 7‚Äì24)

Risk Assessment Enhancement 
Upgrade from 3-tier to 5-tier risk models (adding Critical and Enhanced Medium). AMLA expects a group-wide risk methodology consistent across all EU entities. Banks using sophisticated risk scoring face 60% fewer regulatory challenges.

Technical System Upgrades 
Consolidate fragmented monitoring into a single platform with real-time screening against EU sanctions lists. AMLA mandates EU data residency for all compliance decisions (Frankfurt/Amsterdam). Modern platforms enable this through microservice architectures with API-first design, delivering sub-200ms screening response times.

Organizational Changes 
Implement cross-border coordination procedures between national entities. Enhance whistleblower channels per EU Whistleblower Directive. Conduct quarterly model governance reviews with independent representation. Document due diligence on 100% of AML service providers using AMLA-aligned criteria.

Phase 3: Validation (Final 6 Months)

Operational Readiness 
Conduct a mock AMLA inspection using third-party assessment against the Authority's checklist. Run tabletop exercises for SAR filing timelines. AMLA's 24-hour urgent filing mandate is a massive operational shift requiring on-call investigators and automated workflows.

Training 
Frontline staff need 40+ hours of AMLA-specific training on new documentation standards. Treating this as a checkbox exercise guarantees findings in 2028.
