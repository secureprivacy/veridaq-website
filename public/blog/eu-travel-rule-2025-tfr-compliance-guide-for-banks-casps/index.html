The EU Travel Rule‚Äîofficially the Transfer of Funds Regulation (TFR)‚Äîrequires every bank, payment service provider, and crypto-asset service provider to collect and transmit originator and beneficiary information for all transactions, with no minimum threshold. What Is the EU Transfer of Funds Regulation (TFR)?

The Transfer of Funds Regulation (EU) 2023/1113 is the EU's implementation of FATF Recommendation 16, commonly known as the "Travel Rule." It mandates that payment service providers (PSPs) and crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) collect and transmit complete originator and beneficiary information for every fund transfer and crypto-asset transfer within, into, or out of the EU. This includes names, addresses, account numbers, and distributed ledger addresses where applicable.

Zero-Threshold Requirement

The most significant departure from FATF guidelines is the zero-threshold requirement. While FATF recommends applying the Travel Rule to transactions exceeding ‚Ç¨1,000, the EU TFR applies to all transfers regardless of amount. However, enhanced due diligence and verification obligations only trigger at ‚Ç¨1,000. This means institutions must:

  • Collect originator/beneficiary data for every transaction (no exceptions)
  • Verify accuracy of data for transactions exceeding ‚Ç¨1,000
  • mplement automated systems capable of processing 100% of transactions

Self-Hosted Wallet Provisions

The TFR also addresses crypto transactions from self-hosted wallets (private wallets not managed by CASPs). When transactions exceeding ‚Ç¨1,000 originate from self-hosted wallets and interact with hosted wallets managed by CASPs, beneficiary CASPs must collect originator information. This provision closes a significant compliance gap exploited for money laundering. Notabene TFR guide

Who Must Comply: Banks, Fintechs, and Crypto Exchanges

Obliged Entities

The TFR applies to:

  • Payment Service Providers (PSPs): Banks, e-money institutions, payment institutions offering fund transfers
  • Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs): Exchanges, wallet providers, custodians, and any entity offering crypto transfer services
  • Intermediary Providers: Any intermediary facilitating fund or crypto-asset transfers between PSPs or CASPs

All entities‚Äîwhether EU-based or serving EU customers‚Äîmust comply if they process transfers involving EU residents or entities.

EEA Extension

The TFR applies across the European Economic Area (EEA), covering all 27 EU member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway. Non-EU CASPs and PSPs serving European customers must implement TFR-compliant systems for transactions touching EU/EEA jurisdictions. TFR EEA applicability

What's Required Now: Current Compliance Status

As of November 26, 2025, the Transfer of Funds Regulation is fully in effect with all transitional periods closed. Here's what institutions must have operational today:

Technical Infrastructure Requirements (Mandatory Since July 31, 2025)

All CASPs and PSPs must now have production-grade systems capable of:

  • Automated real-time collection of originator and beneficiary data for every transaction
  • Secure transmission of complete information using encrypted, standardized protocols
  • Immediate detection and flagging of missing, incomplete, or suspicious data
  • Automated transaction rejection when required information cannot be verified
  • Full GDPR compliance for data storage, retention, and subject rights management

Manual workarounds, interim systems, and compensating controls are no longer acceptable. The technical infrastructure grace period that allowed temporary solutions ended on July 31, 2025.

Information Requirements and Data Collection

Under Article 16 of the TFR, beneficiary CASPs and PSPs must receive and validate the following for each transaction:

Originator Information:

  • Full legal name
  • Postal address or national identification number
  • Account number or distributed ledger address
  • Date and place of birth (for transactions exceeding ‚Ç¨1,000)

Beneficiary Information:

  • Full legal name
  • Account number or distributed ledger address

This information must accompany the transfer immediately and securely. Beneficiary institutions must implement systems to detect missing, incomplete, or meaningless data and take appropriate action (execute, reject, return, or suspend). VASPnet TFR guide

How Banks and Exchanges Can Implement Travel Rule Compliance

Technical Infrastructure Requirements

Institutions must deploy systems capable of:

  1. Real-Time Data Collection: Capture originator/beneficiary details at transaction initiation
  2. Secure Data Transmission: Encrypted, standardized protocols for information exchange
  3. Automated Risk Scoring: Multi-factor risk assessment for every transaction
  4. Transaction Monitoring: Detect missing, incomplete, or suspicious data patterns
  5. GDPR Compliance: Secure storage, limited retention, data subject rights management

Next Steps for EU Institutions

As of November 26, 2025, the July 31, 2025 technical infrastructure deadline has passed. Institutions must ensure they are in full compliance:

  1. Conduct Immediate Compliance Audit: Verify current systems meet TFR Article 16 requirements without exceptions

  3. Validate Technical Infrastructure: Ensure real-time data collection and transmission systems are fully operational
  4. Verify Staff Readiness: Confirm compliance, operations, and IT teams understand current TFR obligations
  5. Test System Performance: Validate data accuracy, transmission security, and rejection protocols under production conditions
  6. Document Full Compliance: Maintain comprehensive audit trails demonstrating TFR compliance for AMLA review
